Network Administration Project – Reflection

The Network Configuration and Systems Administration project represents a meaningful application of the technical and ethical principles developed throughout my coursework. The project demonstrates practical skills in configuring network infrastructure, managing core services such as DHCP and DNS, and ensuring reliable connectivity across systems. More importantly, it reflects my growing understanding of how technical decisions directly affect security, trust, and long-term system sustainability.

One of the key challenges addressed during refinement was improving clarity, scalability, and maintainability. While the original configuration met functional requirements, the project was strengthened through clearer documentation and improved structural organization. These refinements ensure the system can be easily understood and maintained by other administrators, reducing operational risk and supporting long-term continuity.

Ethical considerations were intentionally integrated through improved access control and accountability. Administrative privileges were refined to align with the principle of least privilege, ensuring users only have access necessary for their responsibilities. Documentation practices were also enhanced to promote transparency, traceability, and responsible system management.

These refinements had a direct impact on both technical quality and ethical alignment. The system became more secure, scalable, and maintainable, while also reflecting professional responsibility and trustworthiness. This project demonstrates how ethical leadership in IT is expressed through everyday configuration and design decisions.

Overall, this experience strengthened my ability to approach IT work with intentionality, discipline, and accountability. It reinforced the idea that effective technology solutions require both technical expertise and ethical awareness, preparing me to serve organizations and communities responsibly through technology.

Ethical Dilemma Reflection

One ethical dilemma that I ponder on during my IT studies is the abuse of administrative privileges. This is because those authorized to use administrative privileges have the highest access to sensitive information and may want to use such information for their own convenience or gain. While technical controls such as audits and logs are important, the main factor remains the personal integrity of the individual.

The approach I will take in the future when handling similar situations is based on accountability, self-discipline, and agency. Doctrine and Covenants 58:27–28 teaches that we are accountable for our own choices. This means acting ethically even when no one is watching. Ethical behavior is not a restriction but a responsibility.

Reflecting on this ethical dilemma has strengthened my understanding that technical skills alone are not sufficient without integrity. As a follower of Jesus Christ, I am accountable not only to employers and users but also to the Lord. This realization strengthens my commitment to using my skills to protect and serve others rather than to take advantage of them.
